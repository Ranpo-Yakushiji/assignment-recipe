Corned Beef and Cabbage

Ingredients:
  1. 3 pounds corned beef brisket with spice packet
  2. 10 small red potatoes
  3. 5 carrots, peeled and cut into 3-inch pieces
  4. 1 large head cabbage, cut into small wedges

How To Do?
  1. Place corned beef in large pot or Dutch oven and cover with water. Add the spice packet that came with the corned beef. Cover pot and bring to a boil, then reduce to a simmer. Simmer approximately 50 minutes per pound or until tender.
  2. Add whole potatoes and carrots, and cook until the vegetables are almost tender. Add cabbage and cook for 15 more minutes. Remove meat and let rest 15 minutes.
  3. Place vegetables in a bowl and cover. Add as much broth (cooking liquid reserved in the Dutch oven or large pot) as you want. Slice meat across the grain.

Monkfish Risotto with Matchasabi

Ingredients:
  1. 300g/10oz monkfish fillet, cut into small pieces
  2. sea salt and freshly ground black pepper
  3. 50g/2oz/ 1/4 cup butter
  4. 1 large onion, finely chopped
  5. 2 garlic cloves, crushed
  6. 1 tsp wasabi paste
  7. 1 tsp matcha
  8. approx 1 litre/35fl oz/ 4 1/4 cups hot fish stock
  9. 250g/9oz/generous 1 cup risotto rice
  10. 150ml/5fl oz/ 2/3 cup dry white wine
  11. 3 tbsp chopped fresh parsley or chervil
How To Do?
  1. Season the fish with salth and pepper.Melt half the butter in a large saucepan and gently fry the onion for 2 minutes. Add the garlic and fish, and cook gently for 2 minutes. Lift the fish out onto a plate.
  2. Whisk the wasabi and matcha woth a few tablespoons of the stock in a small bowl and reserve.
  3. Add the rice to the pan and cook, strring, for 1 minute. Pour in the wine and let it bubble until almost completely evaporated. Stir in a ladleful of the hot stock and cook until absorbed. Continue to cook, adding the stock a ladleful at a time and stirring for a couple of minutes before adding more stock. Cook until the rice has softened but retains a little bite. It should be quite juicy-you might not need all the stock.
  4. Return the fish to the pan with the matcha mixture and parsley or chervil. Dot in the remaining butter, mix well until the butter melts and season to taste before serving.
